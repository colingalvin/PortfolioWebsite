From auditions behind a screen
to portfolios on a screen

Showing what it takes when it matters

Over my years of preparing for and taking professional music auditions, I have become intimiately familiar with the most nerve-racking aspect auditions: making the most of the opportunity.

Often, taking an auditions means spending hundreds of dollars on travel and hotel accomodations and thousands of hours of practice to gain 5 minutes of attention from a panel of musicians and potential employers, sitting patiently behind a blind curtain, and keenly awaiting the slightest excuse to keep you from the next round. Failing to execute when given the opportunity can lead to dire financial and mental straits. This leads all successful musicians inevitably to the same conclusion - don't waste a moment in preparation to show the best you have.

I'm still in that business, though now my potential employers sit behind a computer screen instead of a curtain hung in the middle of the room. Rest assured, I still intend to waste no opportunity to show the best the I have to offer.
